Technology

The OpenFlexure platform

A fully open-source microscope combining 3D-printed flexure mechanics, laser-cut structural panels, and accessible open electronics — delivering sub-micron positioning at a fraction of the cost of commercial instruments.

01

3D printed flexure mechanics

Every structural and kinematic component prints on a standard FDM printer. Flexure-based XYZ positioning achieves sub-100 nm step sizes without any precision machining.

FDM printing Sub-100 nm steps
02

Laser-cut structural panels

Rigid panels in acrylic or plywood provide dimensional accuracy and mechanical strength, combining with printed flexures for a precision-fit assembly that needs no glue or special tooling.

Acrylic / plywood ±0.1 mm tolerance
03

Open electronics — Arduino & Raspberry Pi

Widely available, community-supported boards at the core. Fully programmable with Python, no proprietary controllers, no vendor lock-in. Swap, upgrade, or extend at any time.

Arduino Raspberry Pi Python API
04

Field-repairable by design

Every broken component can be reprinted or recut on-site. With a total parts cost under $150, repair is faster and cheaper than any service contract — anywhere in the world.

OSHWA certified CERN-OHL-S
Mission & values

Science without barriers.

Cutting-edge research should not be the exclusive domain of well-funded institutions. We build tools that are accessible by design — from material cost to documentation to repairability.

Accessible

High-performance instruments designed for laboratories with limited budgets — from secondary schools to field research stations in remote or low-resource regions around the world.

Target unit cost < $150 USD

Sustainable

Manufacture locally, repair indefinitely. Digital fabrication eliminates complex supply chains — reducing cost, carbon footprint, and dependency on international logistics.

100% locally fabricable parts

Open

Every design file, firmware source, and protocol is published openly. Science advances faster when knowledge is shared without restriction or license barriers.

CERN-OHL-S licensed hardware
